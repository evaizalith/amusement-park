Geauga Lake 2

"Rides & Experiences"

The greatest in all things amusement and park!

Here at Geauga Lake 2, we have tons of rides, shops, and other attractions for you to choose from to spend your day at! Here are some of them now, and keep in mind that there are DEFINITELY more rides at the actual park! Because we TOTALLY have the budget for that!

Daredevil's Dash

We'll be frank. This roller coaster violates several safety guidelines. Humans were never meant to move as fast as they do on this ride. But hey, there isn't any gain without a little pain, right? NOTE: Safety waver signature required for those who wish to ride.

Rampaging Reagan

This exciting roller coaster features thrills, chills, and trickle down economics! Jump in a lightning-fast roller coaster car and run away from 40th United States President Ronald Reagan as fast as you can before he defunds your community!

The Lagoon Arcade

Welcome to the Geauga Lagoon Arcade, a place full of games, food, and prizes! Many guests have spent their entire visit in this place, and why wouldn't they? It has everything you need to stay here, forever, and ever, and ever, and ever!

Literally just the Toadstool Cafe from that new Mario theme park at Universal Studios

Our marketing team has learned of a new, hardly-touched demographic of people: terminally online redditors. We have created a 1 to 1 copy of the actual Toadstool Cafe in hopes of getting those lazy bums out of their cave. You may be wondering, "How did you get the rights to this?" And the answer to that is simple: we didn't. Please don't tell anyone.

Robin's Wild Ride

I don't know HOW this got here, I don't know WHAT it does, and I don't know WHY it exists. We literally were making the park one day and then: bam! This thing shows up out of nowhere with some random guy in the booth. Even though it's run down, people can still (barely) ride it. Additionally, the man in the control booth is both irritable and unhelpful, often getting upset at people when they ask to ride, and frequently sending people on an accidental second lap of the ride. We can't even ask him to leave since the booth he is in is seemingly indestructible and he just shuts the door whenever somebody tries to talk to him. Only reason why he's still here is because the higher ups think we can market this as some "crazy mystery!" but honestly, this ride is doing more harm than good.
